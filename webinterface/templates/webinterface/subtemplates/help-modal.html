Info / about

UNDER CONSTRUCTION: This website is currently under development by the Marine Robotics group at the Australian Centre for Field Robotics. It is intended to provide an easy to use system that facilitates exploration and management of AUV data, improved annotation interfaces and access to our suite of machine learning tools, including clustering, supervised classification and automated percentage cover estimation. It is still a work in progress, and at this stage we have mainly been focussing on the annotation interface. There may still be a few bugs and todos, so any feedback and comments would be greatly appreciated.

Current features/tools:
  • Advanced map-based project creation interface. Create projects from AUV deployment(s) using filter search tools on depth, altitude, data or bounding boxes
  • Subset images in the project using temporally stratified (every Nth image) or random selection (N random images)
  • Set up annotation runs using N random points
  • Easy to use annotation interface with searchable tags, zoom tool, marquee multiple select tool, and other useful tools.
  • Basic map interface - once you have a dataset selected, you can view the selected project/subset/image on a map.

Proposed future features/tools:
  • Data exploration interface: explore all the data and the labels in the system through a map with the ability to view relevant images and annotation information. Similar to the current IMOS data portal, but with more information, including image mosaics underlaid, multibeam bathy and classification/annotation information.
  • Spatial sampling techniques for sub-setting images including spatial stratified, GRTS, etc...
  • Automated clustering tool to help with selecting images for further analysis
  • Supervised classification tools to help automate the labelling of points/pixels and extrapolating percentage coverage estimation.
  • Data import and export tools, and reporting tools for summaries and statistics: make it easy to import existing labeled data from CPCe (and other common programs).
  • Ability to export annotation and survey data in formats suitable for further analysis.
  • Image brightness and contrast adjustment in image viewer.

About:
  • IMOS was established in 2007 and includes participants from all of Australia’s main university and government research organizations involved in marine science research. IMOS is designed to be a fully-integrated, national system, observing at ocean-basin and regional scales, and covering physical, chemical and biological variables.
  • IMOS brings together researchers from a variety of institutions and disciplines. Represented within this group are benthic ecologists, geoscientists, computer scientists and engineers from multiple institutions who have come together by the shared understanding that a collaborative approach is essential for effective marine habitat monitoring at a national scale. Collaborators include the Australian Centre for Field Robotics (ACFR) at Sydney University; the Institute for Marine and Antarctic Studies (IMAS) at the University of Tasmania; the Sydney Institute of Marine Science (SIMS) at the University of New South Wales; the Oceans Institute at the University of Western Australia; CSIRO Marine and Atmospheric Research; and the Australian Institute for Marine Science.
  • IMOS is supported by the Department of Industry, Innovation, Climate Change, Science, Research and Tertiary Education; the National Environmental Research Program Marine Biodiversity Hub; the Australian Research Council; the Department of Sustainability, Environment, Water, Population and Communities; the Australian National Data Service (ANDS); NSW DPI Fisheries; Department of Fisheries WA; Department of Primary Industries, Parks, Water and Environment.

Help / How to

How to sign in:
  • In order to create projects and view annotations, you need to sign in.
  • If you are not signed in, you will see a red icon on the top right of the nav bar. Click it to sign in or create an account (you will have to close this modal window first).

How to access data:
  • Select/create project, subset and annotation run by clicking the dropdown link in the top nav bar.
  • Create subsets and annotation runs by clicking the grey buttons at the bottom of the "Choose a Dataset" modal window.

How to annotate images:
  • Once you have an annotation run selected (using the steps above), you will see a list of thumbnails in the "View Images" tab on the top nav bar.
  • Click one of the thumnails to open the image viewer.
  • The nav bar on the image viewer provided access to "Image Info", "Image Tools" and "Annotation Tools"
  • Click the "Annotation Tools" button to start annotating. The nav bar in the annotation pane provides additional options.
